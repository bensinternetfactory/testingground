# Tow Loans — Homepage Landing Page Breakdown

## Tow Truck Financing Built for the Industry

**Core Idea:** Tow truck financing from a team that only finances tow trucks. We know the equipment, we know the business, and we don't waste your time.

**Psychology:** Tow operators are tired of explaining their business to generic lenders. A financing company that already speaks their language earns trust fast.

**Target Customer:** Towing company owner running 3–15 trucks, turning down calls because they need more equipment on the road. Every day without another truck is revenue lost.

**Business Model:** Tow truck financing broker. Rollbacks, flatbeds, wreckers, heavy-duty rotators — new and used. Pre-approval in under 30 seconds with no credit check. Soft pull only if they move forward with an application. 24-hour approvals. Rates built around towing equipment.

## Section 1: Sticky Navigation Bar

### Navigation Links

- **Tow Truck Financing** — primary path, links to hero CTA or application
- **Truck Types** — anchor link to Quick Navigation Cards (Section 3)
- **Programs** — anchor link to Financing Program Cards (Section 5)
- **Calculator** — anchor link to Calculator CTA (Section 8)
- **Reviews** — anchor link to Social Proof (Section 9)

### Phone Number

**1-800-XXX-XXXX** displayed with a clean truck icon — always visible, click-to-call on mobile. No "call us" label. Tow operators are phone people. Make the number big enough to tap between calls.

### CTA Button (Top Right)

**"Get Pre-Approved in 30 Seconds"**

Short, direct, low-commitment. "Pre-approved" tells them they'll get a real answer. "30 seconds" removes the time objection — they dispatch faster than that.

### Logo

Tow Loans wordmark positioned left. Clean, professional. A subtle hook or boom element is fine, but no clip-art wreckers. This is a financing company that knows towing, not a tow company that dabbles in lending.

### Behavior

Sticky on scroll. On mobile, collapses to hamburger but keeps the phone number and CTA visible at all times. The phone number never hides.

> **Why this works:** Every nav link speaks towing. No "Auto Loans" or "Equipment Financing." Before they read a word of body copy, the navigation says: this entire operation exists for tow operators.

## Section 2: Hero Section

### Eyebrow Text

`TOW TRUCK FINANCING — BUILT FOR THE INDUSTRY`

All caps, small, above the headline. Sets the frame. Puts the target keyword front and center.

### Headline

**"Tow Truck Financing From People Who Know the Business"**

### Subheadline

**"We've financed over 3,000 tow trucks — flatbeds, rollbacks, wreckers, and heavy-duty rotators. Get pre-approved in under 30 seconds. No credit check. Just a straight answer."**

### CTA Button

**"Get Pre-Approved — No Credit Check"**

Large button, high contrast. Below the subheadline. One action. Below the button, microcopy in a smaller font:

*"30-second form. No credit impact. Real pre-approval — not a bait quote."*

### Image / Visual Direction

A high-quality, editorial-style photo of a late-model rollback on a real job — not a studio shot. Think: truck on the shoulder of a highway at golden hour, light bar off, driver walking toward the cab. The truck should be clean but working. It should look like money being made. No stock photography. The target customer will clock a fake setup instantly.

Alternative: A split composition — left half is a sharp photo of a heavy-duty rotator mid-recovery (the aspirational truck), right half fades into the CTA area.

> **Why this works:** The headline is specific without being showy. It tells tow operators this page is for them — not general contractors, not trucking companies. The subheadline stacks credibility (3,000 trucks), names the equipment correctly, and immediately addresses two objections: how long does this take (30 seconds) and will it hit my credit (no). The Tow Loans voice is direct and honest. No games.

## Section 4: Rate Ticker

**Intentionally omitted.**

Tow truck financing rates vary based on truck age, mileage, credit profile, and whether it's a new build or used unit. Displaying a generic rate invites comparison shopping against products that aren't comparable. The strategy is to get them into a conversation. Tow Loans wins on specialization and trust — not by posting the lowest APR on the page.
