# Angle 1: The Revenue Leak

## Landing Page Breakdown — Tow Truck Financing

**Core idea:** Every call you can't cover is cash driving straight to your competitor's yard. The truck pays for itself. You just need the financing to move before the opportunity does.

**Psychology:** The monthly payment isn't a cost — it's a rounding error against the revenue that truck generates. The enemy isn't debt. It's the empty bay, the unanswered call, the dispatch board lighting up with work you can't take. Operators feel this every single day.

**Headline direction:** "How Many Calls Did You Turn Down Last Week?"

**Target customer:** Established towing company owner running 3-15 trucks who's turning down calls because they don't have enough iron on the road. They're losing revenue every day they wait.

**CTA flow:** Pre-approve (30 seconds, no hard credit pull) → See estimated payments → Apply if the numbers work.

A $1,200/month payment feels like a cost. A $1,200/month payment against $8,000-$15,000/month in gross revenue per truck feels like a no-brainer. The secondary CTA below nudges them toward pre-approval — because estimated payments are fine, but actual payments based on their profile are better. That's the bridge from calculator to pre-approval to application.

## CTA Funnel Summary

Every CTA on this page routes to the same three-step flow:

**Step 1: Pre-Approve (30 seconds)**
Short form. No hard credit pull. Operator gets a straight answer on what they qualify for.

**Step 2: See Your Payment**
Based on their pre-approval, they see estimated monthly payments for the equipment and terms that fit their profile. Real numbers, not generic estimates.

**Step 3: Apply**
If the payment works, they move to a full application. This is where the hard credit check happens. No surprises — they already know the ballpark.

This flow respects their time and earns their trust. They're never more than 30 seconds from knowing where they stand, and they never commit to anything until they've seen the numbers.
