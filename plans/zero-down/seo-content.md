## Why Zero Down Is a Business Decision, Not a Handout

Most lenders treat a down payment like a trust exercise. Put skin in the game, prove you're serious. That logic makes sense for consumer auto loans. It falls apart in towing.

Here's the math operators already know:

A single tow truck averaging 2-3 calls per day at $200+ per tow generates $12,000-$18,000/month in gross revenue. A typical monthly payment on a financed truck runs $1,500-$1,600. The truck covers its own payment within the first week of the month.

So why would you drain $10,000-$25,000 in working capital for a down payment when the truck starts earning before the first bill hits?

That cash has better places to be:

- **Operating reserves** — one insurance renewal or major repair bill and you're scrambling
- **Growth capital** — the next truck, the next contract, the next opportunity that shows up on a Tuesday with no warning
- **Payroll** — drivers don't wait for your cash flow to catch up
- **Equipment** — new bed, new wheel lift, GPS, lighting — the stuff that keeps trucks road-ready

Zero down isn't about qualifying for less. It's about deploying capital where it works hardest.

## How to Qualify for $0 Down

No guesswork. There are two paths. You probably already fit one of them.

### Path A: Credit and Comparable Debt

Three things:

1. **640+ credit score** — not perfect credit. Not 700. Just 640.
2. **2 years in business** — you've survived the hardest part. That matters.
3. **Comparable debt** — you've made at least 12 payments on an auto or installment loan worth at least half the amount you're borrowing.

That last one trips people up, so here's what it means in plain terms:

If you're financing a $100,000 truck, we want to see that you've made 12 consecutive payments on a loan of $50,000 or more. Could be a truck you already own. Could be equipment. Could be a personal vehicle loan. It proves you can handle the payment — and that's what lets us drop the down payment to zero.

### Path B: Fleet Operator

Three things:

1. **640+ credit score** — same bar
2. **2 years in business** — same bar
3. **3+ tow trucks in your fleet** — you're already running an operation. The fleet itself is the proof.

If you're running three trucks, you know how to manage payments, insurance, maintenance, and drivers. That track record replaces the need for comparable debt.

**Don't fit either path exactly?** Apply anyway. These are the two fastest routes to $0 down, but we structure every deal individually. Your situation might qualify through a different combination.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does zero down mean higher interest rates?

No. Your rate is based on your credit profile and business history, not your down payment amount. $0 down doesn't mean you pay a premium — as the example above shows, the rate is the same whether you put money down or not.

### Can I still put money down if I want to?

Yes. Some operators prefer a lower monthly payment and choose to put 10-20% down. The option is yours. We don't require it.

### What credit score do I actually need?

640 is the baseline for both qualification paths. If you're close but not quite there, apply anyway — we look at the full picture, not just the number.

### How fast is the approval process?

Pre-approval takes less than 30 seconds and does not impact your credit score. Full approval and funding typically takes 2 hours after you submit your application depending on the time of day you submit it.

### Do you finance trucks from private sellers?

Yes. Dealer, private party, auction — the source doesn't matter. Same terms, same zero-down availability regardless of where the truck comes from.

### What's "comparable debt" exactly?

It means you've made at least 12 on-time payments on an auto loan or installment loan that's worth at least half of what you're looking to borrow. If you're borrowing $80,000, we want to see 12 payments on a $40,000+ loan. It's proof you can manage the payment — that's all.

### Is there a maximum loan amount with zero down?

We finance tow trucks from $25,000 to $3,000,000+. Zero down is available across the full range for qualified operators.
